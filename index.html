The Godmode Algorithm

How We Measure True Strength

The Problem

Most fitness apps use a simplistic approach: they track the heaviest weight you touched that day. This is flawed. It ignores volume, rep ranges, and consistency. It rewards ego-lifting rather than working capacity.

Our Solution

Godmode uses a deterministic approach to tracking. We don't guess; we calculate. We utilize an Aggregate Estimated 1-Rep Max (e1RM) to generate a single, stable performance score for every session.

The Core Formula: Epley e1RM

e1RM = w × (1 + r / 30)

where w = weight lifted, r = repetitions

Example: 100kg × 10 reps = 133.3kg theoretical 1-rep max

Why This Works

Averaging the top 3 sets removes volatility. If you hit a PR on set 1 but burn out on the rest, your score reflects that drop. It rewards consistent performance across multiple working sets—the exact type of training required for hypertrophy.

"If the line goes up, your muscle has no choice but to grow."
